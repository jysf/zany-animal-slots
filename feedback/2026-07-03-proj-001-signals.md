## meta

**Re-flagged but never adopted or rejected (the highest-value process-debt).** Three
items were raised *before PROJ-001 even started* — in the `2026-04-20` bragfile
round — and re-surfaced unresolved months later: `archive-spec-no-backlog-edit`
(April → dogfood#9 → retrospective, three rounds over ~2.5 months),
`repo-id-placeholder-lag`, and the whole `first-class-multiagent-mode` direction
("design for multi-agent variants," April → dogfood#11/#14 → retrospective's
"richest new learning"). But the sharper meta-signal is a *disposition* gap, not a
capture gap: the template captures findings well (per-spec/stage reflections) yet has
no mechanism to adopt-or-reject them. `perf60-not-in-references` and
`a11y-perf-invariants-unnamed` were each deferred to "the weekly review" 3–5 times and
never dispositioned; `license-policy-severity-lag` was flagged in **six** documents and
never executed during the project. "Defer to the weekly review" became a black hole.
**The template needs a triage/disposition step that forces every recurring reflection
note to open|adopt|reject — otherwise findings accrete without ever landing.**

**Friction a generic template can't encode.** The `cold-agent-toolprior-drift` cluster
is the clearest: a template can't know *this repo's* ESLint plugin set, whether
`@testing-library/user-event` is installed, or that `scripts/*.mjs` run under Node —
those are per-repo toolchain truths. Likewise `verify-hosting-product-before-deploy-frame`
(the template can't know what Cloudflare offers this account this month) and
`verify-dropin-import-paths` (can't validate a specific spec's imports). The
generalizable conclusion: **the template can encode structure and gates, but not
repo-specific toolchain truth or external-platform state.** The right move is a *slot* —
a required, per-instance "toolchain brief" that build prompts inject — rather than
trying to hard-code the gotchas upstream.

**If this ran on a non-Claude agent, what would break or be awkward.** Four
Claude-coupled seams: (1) **Cost/metering** — `tokens_total` is captured from the Agent
result's `subagent_tokens` / `/cost`; another platform may not expose per-run token
counts the same way, so `cost-audit` (a hard gate) would have no source. (2)
**Model-id + pricing fields** — specs hard-code `claude-opus-4-8` / `claude-sonnet-4-6`
in `agents.*` and cost sessions, and estimate at `$6.6/M Sonnet`; both the ids and the
rate are Claude-specific and would be wrong on GPT/Gemini. (3) **Model-tier prompt
wording** — the design=Opus / build=Sonnet strategy ("Run on claude-sonnet-4-6, AGENTS
§8") assumes the Claude model family; the `set-subagent-model-explicitly` lesson
(defaulting to Opus) is a Claude-Agent-SDK behavior. (4) **Sub-agent mechanics** — the
`shared-tree-subagent-corruption` hazard is specific to the Agent-tool substrate; a
different orchestrator might isolate agents (or fail differently). The portable parts
survive intact — `trust-git-disk-over-self-report` is agent-agnostic. **Fix:
parameterize model ids, the $/M rate, and the metering source behind config, and make
the model-tier map pluggable.**

**What the discipline bought vs. what felt ceremonial.** *Bought:* the headline ROI is
the engine freezing at SPEC-011 and never changing through 26 more specs — the
failing-tests-first + DEC-001 boundary-test discipline made the engine/presentation
separation real, not aspirational. `contract-tests-as-guards` turned subjective juice
into CI (an unexpected win that refuted the project's own risk thesis). The
`value.thesis`/`value_contribution` blocks caught scope creep up front. And the
cost-audit gate + `trust-git-disk` rule caught two real sub-agent failures (SPEC-004
truncation, SPEC-036 kill) that self-reports would have waved through. *Ceremonial:*
the per-spec 3+3 reflection questions produced near-duplicate "no updates needed"
answers on small mechanical specs — value concentrated in a handful of specs, the rest
were rote. The `tokens_total: null` + boilerplate-note bookkeeping on every design/ship
cycle across 37 specs is pure ritual. The `[~]`/`[x]` timeline markers were not just
low-value but actively *misleading* (they lied in the SPEC-004 failure). And full
design→build→verify→ship + one-spec-per-PR for one-line `[REPO]` config specs felt
disproportionate to the change. Net: **the ceremony paid off exactly when it produced
an enforceable artifact (a test, a gate, a decision) and felt like tax when it produced
prose.**
